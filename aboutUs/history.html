Introduction

The Department of Agriculture, Cooperation & Farmers Welfare (DAC&FW) has a rich history dating back to 1871. Over the years, it has evolved to cater to the diverse needs of Indian agriculture and its allied sectors.

Pre-Independence Period

In June 1871, the Department of Revenue and Agriculture and Commerce was established to focus on agricultural resource development. Earlier, agriculture was under the Home Department. In 1881, the Famine Commission suggested the formation of a separate Agriculture Department, and it was officially reconstituted. By 1923, it merged with Education, Health, and Lands departments to improve efficiency.

Post-Independence Period

After India's independence in 1947, the Ministry of Agriculture emerged as a distinct entity. Over the years, it saw multiple reorganizations:

  • 1951-1957: Mergers with the Ministry of Food and other departments for streamlining operations.
  • 1960s-1980s: Creation of specialized departments like Rural Development, Irrigation, and Cooperation to tackle agricultural challenges.
  • 1990s: Establishment of the Department of Animal Husbandry & Dairying to focus on livestock and dairy development.

Recent Developments

In 2015, the Ministry was renamed as the Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare. It was reorganized into two key departments:

  • Department of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare (DA&FW)
  • Department of Agricultural Research and Education (DARE)

In 2021, the Cooperation Division was carved out to form the Ministry of Cooperation, further streamlining its operations.

Current Structure

The Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare now oversees a wide range of activities through its two departments and multiple autonomous bodies, working to enhance agricultural productivity and farmer welfare across the nation.
