1. Promoting Sustainable Agriculture

The government emphasizes sustainable farming practices to conserve natural resources, enhance soil health, and ensure long-term productivity. Policies focus on encouraging organic farming and reducing chemical dependency.

2. Enhancing Farmer Income

Initiatives are in place to double farmer income through market reforms, subsidies, and crop diversification. Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) schemes ensure financial assistance reaches farmers directly.

3. Adoption of Technology

Encouraging the use of modern technologies such as precision farming, AI-driven tools, and drone-based crop monitoring to enhance efficiency and productivity.

4. Irrigation and Water Management

Policies aim to ensure efficient water usage through drip and sprinkler irrigation systems and promoting rainwater harvesting to combat water scarcity issues.

5. Market Access and Export Promotion

Support is provided to farmers for accessing domestic and international markets. The aim is to strengthen agricultural exports by improving quality and meeting global standards.

6. Addressing Climate Change

Programs are designed to help farmers adapt to climate change by promoting climate-resilient crops, sustainable practices, and disaster management training.

7. Rural Infrastructure Development

Investments are being made in rural infrastructure, including roads, storage facilities, and cold chains, to support agricultural supply chains and reduce post-harvest losses.

8. Financial Inclusion for Farmers

Efforts are underway to provide easy access to credit and insurance schemes to protect farmers against crop failure and market risks.
